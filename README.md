# 📜 Decoding the Book of Soyga

A verification-first research repository for manuscript analysis, canonical corpus building, and computational testing on the **Book of Soyga**.

This repository documents an ongoing effort to move Soyga research from broad symbolic speculation toward **checkable structure**, **witness-aware reconstruction**, and **falsifiable computational analysis**.

---

## ✨ Project Focus

The current phase of the project concentrates on one central question:

**Does the Book of Soyga preserve mechanically recoverable structure, especially in its table-naming system, rather than only symbolic or opaque content?**

The strongest verified results so far suggest that **Section 28** contains a real internal naming mechanism, especially in its opening block, where table names appear to be generated from source strings and paired through explicit mirrored reversal.

---

## 🔍 Current Research Position

This project does **not** claim a final decryption of the Book of Soyga.

What it does claim is more careful and more useful:

- ✅ a mechanically framed mirrored engine is strongly supported in the opening run of Section 28
- ✅ a verified corpus has been built from local manuscript-based sources
- ✅ a strict high-confidence subset has been isolated for computational testing
- ✅ multiple compact-law families have already been tested and rejected
- ✅ the project now distinguishes clearly between:
  - exact anchors
  - variant-supported anchors
  - mixed-transition material
  - unstable late material

This repository is therefore best read as a **research supplement and verification archive**, not a final-answer repository.

---

## 🧭 Verified Structural Model

At the current stage, the best-supported zone model is:

### 1. Core Mirrored Engine
**Entries 1–12**

This is the strongest part of Section 28.  
It preserves explicit reversal syntax and mirrored table-name pairing.

### 2. Mixed Transition Zone
**Entries 13–17**

The mirrored surface weakens, but parts of the extraction structure appear to continue.

### 3. Unstable Late Zone
**Entries 18–23**

Witness instability, corruption risk, and structural uncertainty are much higher here.

## 🧪 Computational Method

This research combines:

- 📖 close manuscript reading
- 🧾 witness-aware corpus extraction
- 🐍 Python-based reconstruction and testing
- 🧠 recent LLMs used as analytical assistants
- ✅ verification-first filtering of all proposed results

Large language models were used as **research assistants**, not as authorities.
They helped accelerate extraction, comparison, organization, and hypothesis generation, but no result is treated as accepted unless it survives manuscript-based and computational verification.

---

## 🚫 What Has *Not* Been Proven

At the current stage:

- no final decryption has been achieved
- no global compact ordinal law has been proven
- no low-complexity exact family has survived strict holdout validation
- several attractive law candidates have already been rejected

This negative result is important.  
It narrows the search space and strengthens the credibility of what remains.

## 📌 Research Philosophy

This project follows a simple rule:

> prefer verification over excitement  
> prefer downgrade over exaggeration  
> prefer falsification over decorative pattern-hunting

That means some earlier ideas remain preserved here even when they were later weakened or rejected. This is intentional. The repository is meant to preserve the **actual research path**, not only its strongest surviving claims.
